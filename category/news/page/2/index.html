SNCL Match Day 1

Posted on 28th October 2024 by Ray Flood

Scottish National Chess League 2024-25, Dundee City Report, Match Day 1, October 27th 2024

 

The new 2024-25 chess season has come upon us fast. This season, Dundee City, a combined team comprising members of both Castlehill Chess Club and Dundee Chess Club, is competing with 4 teams. With each team represented by 5 players, one can imagine how much of a challenge it is getting 20 players to Dunfermline on a rainy day in October – but we did it! This year, we have both our A-team and B-team playing in Division1. Our A-team will be hoping to mount a challenge for the title of national champions while our newly promoted B-team will be doing their best to solidify their place in the top division following promotion last season. Both Dundee City C- and D-teams are competing in Division 3 with each having the hope of challenging for promotion to Division 2. That said, this season we have more players wanting to play than are able to play and a priority will be to give everyone a chance to compete in this prestigious tournament.

And so to Round 1 on Match Day 1. In the top Division as fate would have it, Dundee City A team was paired against our B-team. The result was a thumping 5-0 win for the A-team, a not unpredictable result but a result that underlines the strength of the A-team – they will surely be challenging for the title at the end of the season. In Division 3, results were a bit mixed. The C-team were paired against Hamilton B. There were two nice wins for Matthew Deighan and Tom Wardlaw on Boards 2 and 3. These were added to with draws from Alisdair McLeod and Subhayu Banerjee on Boards 1 and 4 respectively and resulting in a 3-2 win – a nice start. Our D-team were up against Kilmarnock and they ran into difficulties. The only win was from Eve Kenicer on Board 5 and a draw from Steve Thomson on Board 1 – so it was a 1.5-3.5 defeat in the end.

In the afternoon in Division 1, the A-team were up against a strong Stepps team led by IM Doug Bryson on Board 1. As the games unfolded, all except one were drawn. The exception was from Ed Spencer who overcame William Hulme. William was trying to salvage a draw with perpetual checks on the White King. But Ed had seen through all the lines and when Black had no more checks the game was over. Of note was a heavyweight tussle in a Winawer French on Board 1 between Doug Bryson and our own Jacques Ophoff. In the ending, Jacques was an exchange ahead but the position was sufficiently blocked that neither side could make progress. So a 3-2 win for our A-team and 4 match points at close of play.

The B-team had to face Forth Valley A in the afternoon. We knew that they were a very strong team because they had excelled in Division 1 the previous season and had given our A-team a run for their money a year ago. With the B-team strengthened by the addition of Gary Weir on Board 1 and Stephen Hogg on Board 2, a fierce battle was guaranteed. With draws from Gary Weir, Stephen Hogg and also Andre Babin on Board 3, the outcome of the match would depend on what happened on Boards 4 and 5. The outcome was everything we hoped for. First Joshua Crofts overcame Daniel Kitaev on Board 4 followed soon after with a win by Alastair Dawson over James Shemilt on bottom board. So the B-team walked away with a fantastic win and two match points that certainly made up for the trauma they encountered during the morning round!

In Division 3 in the afternoon, our C-team faced the multi-talented Lenzie Dementors, the latter made up of some very promising junior players – and it did not go well. The only points gained were draws for Matthew Deighan, Tom Wardlaw and Colin Young resulting in a 1.5-3.5 loss. It goes without saying that Lenzie will be one of the teams challenging for the Division 3 title at the end of the season. Our D-team fared much better. They faced Edinburgh 3 in the afternoon and they turned on the style. There were convincing wins for Kam Wan, Matthew Hunter, Francesco Polignano and Eve Kenicer. In the end, therefore, it was a 4-1 victory and 2 valuable match points.

Alastair Dawson

Dundee Chess Congress 24

Posted on 14th October 2024 by Ray Flood

This year’s Congress had a highly creditable 19 club members participating in the three events. A few of our players were having their first tournament experience and hopefully they all enjoyed it and it will stand them in good stead for their future ‘careers’.

First thing to say is a huge Thank You to the organisation team behind the Congress who made it all possible. The inestimable Ray Noble was Tournament Director for the first time after so many years of service by the legendary Keith Rose, and Ray handled everything with his usual thorough professionalism and limitless work rate to ensure a great experience was had by all. Ray was ably assisted by his team including Keith, Daniel Coleman and our own Jim Anderson acting as Controller of the Minor. Also the Congress Committee who meet and work throughout the year to ensure everything is going smoothly. None of this would happen without them.

We append the bare statistics of our players who were participating, which of course do not tell the whole story!

Premier

Dundee legend David was our top finisher in the top event with a sole loss to tournament winner Andrew Greet. David characterised his score as “2 ½/4” due to a no-show by IM Megalios in the last round. A big disappointment for David who naturally wants to play the top opposition at every opportunity, but these things happen. Fellow legend Ed had an average Congress by his exceedingly high standards, but just one loss to IM Sarakauskas, which shows the enormous strength of this year’s entry. Josh and Jan eschewed the Major in order to enhance their ratings, and will be pleased to have done just that, and Josh taking a grading prize home too! Two draws for Alastair, but his rating performance again shows the quality of opposition he faced.

Major

Vlad was top performer as could be expected, in another very strong tournament. A mixed weekend by his standards, but a combative one with three wins, a draw and just one loss to an up and coming star who is clearly undergraded. It pays to remember that improving underrated juniors are always waiting for us! Another fine performance by Ana to boost her rating, unbeaten till the last round, and facing very tough opposition. Steve also faced a tough field, and will be happy with his weekend. Adam and Bill may be a little disappointed, but Bill is rusty competition-wise, and Adam never quite got going as he’d have liked, but just one loss. Keith’s rating performance from 2 points again shows the quality of his opponents!

Minor

Our breakout star Kat lost her first and last games, but won the middle three to take the grading prize in the Minor! Her first tournament since she was a wee lass, and a cover girl then as she showed us! She takes the spot as our Star Player of the Congress, and her superb rating performance is testament to her progress since she started coming to the club. She owes it all to our tuition of course(!?) but well played Kat! Christine looked to be sweeping to the title with an opening 3/3 but faltered on the last day as her busy schedule caught up with her. Still an excellent performance. Andrew will be happy with a solid performance, and Gavin with a return to tournament play after a long gap. Our new kids on the block Sam, Steven and Sami got a great introduction to tournaments, and enjoyed the weekend, which is what matters most.

Well done everyone!

The only games I have are my own, none of them really publishable, but at least this one features two club members as Vlad beats me up in the ending

19th Bobby Gow Memorial 30/9/24

Posted on 5th October 2024 by Ray Flood

Dundee Chess Club 10½ – 14½ Castlehill Chess Club

The 19th installment in this annual pre-season challenge, in memory of a real ‘Fun Bobby’ (Friends in-joke!) who graced both clubs for many (but still much too few) years. A record turnout produced an incredible 25 board match, and a highly enjoyable evening as ever. We had several players making their competitive Dundee debut, including some making their competitive debut anywhere. At this early stage of the season the ratings and estimates are understandably all over the place, so don’t let it confuse you!

We did have hopes of doing better this year to end our long losing streak, but Castlehill’s overall strength in depth proved an unclimbable mountain; 9-0 on the bottom boards rather tells its own story. Our top seven boards outgraded their opponents, sometimes considerably, but even then we didn’t entirely have everything going to plan, as some resolute performances picked up several valuable points for the visitors. We got off to a bad start, with just a half-point on the board after the first four results, and we never quite managed to close the gap enough to make it a closer contest.

Nevertheless, some great chess played and games to enjoy below, and this match has always been a case of our two friendly clubs getting together and all members of both getting the chance to enjoy competitive, graded chess in the friendliest atmosphere you’ll find. In that regard, Bobby Gow 19 was perhaps the biggest success ever.

Well done everyone. Congratulations Castlehill!
