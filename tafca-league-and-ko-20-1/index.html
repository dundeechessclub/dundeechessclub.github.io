TAFCA league and KO 20/1

Posted on 23rd January 2025 by Ray Flood

Knockout. Dundee A v Castlehill Giants

On paper a match you’d expect the defending champions to win, though the Castlehill side are clearly better than their allegro gradings would suggest. But the time handicap is a big factor, and we were giving a fair bit away.

1st Round

A good start to take a comfortable lead. Vlad frustratingly (for him!) fell into a snap mate against Josh, Alastair won comfortably and Adam rode the storm of the young prodigy Matthew. Keith slipped up but was giving away a lot of time against Steve rated 1430 in Classical, and even that likely an undergrade! Still, a 3-1 lead but we had to be wary. As the higher graded team we neened to win, as a draw would take Castlehill through on board count.

2nd Round

Dundee A 4-4 Castlehill Giants. Castlehill win on board count.

And so it transpired! Alastair got his second win quickly to leave his side needing just a half point from the remaining three games to go through. But Keith wasn’t having his best night and went down again, and Adam succumbed to the form that took Matthew to the Dundee Major title last October. So for the second week running, Joshua was last man standing and battling to give his team the win. But a determined Captain Vlad wouldn’t be denied twice in one night, and made the most of his time advantage to take his side through. A big disappointment for Dundee, winners two seasons out of the last three, but we must applaud Castlehill for their comeback and wish them well for the rest of the competition.

TAFCA Division Two

Certainly not a bad result for the D Team, but they will have regrets. Kat would have been confident having beaten Mike at the Congress a few months back, and gained a clear advantage early on. But a little tactical slip let the experienced Mike crash through for the win. Our Robert was even better off, a piece up on his opponent, but again slipped the peice back and crumbled thereafter. Very good wins though, for Victor on his TAFCA debut against the experienced John, and Andrew’s experience trumped Leo’s youth to gain the draw. Well played everyone.
