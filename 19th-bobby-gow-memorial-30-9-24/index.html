19th Bobby Gow Memorial 30/9/24

Posted on 5th October 2024 by Ray Flood

The 19th installment in this annual pre-season challenge, in memory of a real ‘Fun Bobby’ (Friends in-joke!) who graced both clubs for many (but still much too few) years. A record turnout produced an incredible 25 board match, and a highly enjoyable evening as ever. We had several players making their competitive Dundee debut, including some making their competitive debut anywhere. At this early stage of the season the ratings and estimates are understandably all over the place, so don’t let it confuse you!

We did have hopes of doing better this year to end our long losing streak, but Castlehill’s overall strength in depth proved an unclimbable mountain; 9-0 on the bottom boards rather tells its own story. Our top seven boards outgraded their opponents, sometimes considerably, but even then we didn’t entirely have everything going to plan, as some resolute performances picked up several valuable points for the visitors. We got off to a bad start, with just a half-point on the board after the first four results, and we never quite managed to close the gap enough to make it a closer contest.

Nevertheless, some great chess played and games to enjoy below, and this match has always been a case of our two friendly clubs getting together and all members of both getting the chance to enjoy competitive, graded chess in the friendliest atmosphere you’ll find. In that regard, Bobby Gow 19 was perhaps the biggest success ever.

Well done everyone. Congratulations Castlehill!
